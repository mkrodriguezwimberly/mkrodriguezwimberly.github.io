Hello! I'm a Physics PhD Candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at UC Irvine studying galaxy evolution - specifically ultra-faint dwarf galaxies, star formation quenching and dynamics in satellite galaxies. Alongside my exciting research, I work to improve the astronomical community for all. Currently I am on the Board of Directors at the Astronomical Society of the Pacific and Director of Peer Mentoring for the Cal-Bridge Scholarship Program.

Research

My work focuses on the quenching of star formation on a galactic scale with investigation methods of combining optical observations and cosmological simulations.

Teaching and Service

I conduct astronomy outreach focusing on underrepresented minorities and primarily with K-12 Special Needs students (including children on the Autism Spectrum and those with Down Syndrome). I have previously instructed Intro to Astronomy Lab at Cal State Long Beach. Additionaly, I am the Junior Board Fellow on the Astronomical Society of the Pacific's Board of Directors, I serve on UCI Physics & Astronomy Inclusive Excellence Committee and their Physics Graduate Caucaus.
